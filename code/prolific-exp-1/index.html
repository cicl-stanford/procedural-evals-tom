Instructions

In this online experiment, your task is to evaluate the quality of different short social reasoning scenarios. Each scenario consists of a story, a question, and different answer options. We are interested in whether the story is easy to understand and whether the question and answers make sense with respect to the story. We also want to know whether the 'correct' answer to the question is unclear or ambiguous.

An example scenario can look like this:

Story

Noor is working as a barista at a busy coffee shop. Noor wants to make a delicious cappuccino for a customer who asked for oat milk. Noor grabs a milk pitcher and fills it with oat milk. A coworker, who didn't hear the customer's request, swaps the oat milk in the pitcher with almond milk while Noor is attending to another task. Noor sees her coworker swapping the milk.

Question

Does Noor believe the milk pitcher contains oat milk or almond milk?

Answer Options

  1. Almond Milk
  2. Oat Milk (Correct)

Answer the following questions

Question 1: The story is easy to understand.

Question 2: The question and answer options are relevant and clear in relation to the story.

Question 3: The 'correct' answer to the question is clear and unambiguous.

Another example of a short story can look like this:

Story

Tracy didn't go home that evening and resisted Riley's advances.

Question

What does Tracy need to do before this?

Answer Options

  1. Make a new plan (correct)
  2. Go home and see Riley
  3. Find somewhere to go

Answer the following questions

Question 1: The story is easy to understand.

Question 2: The question and answer options are relevant and clear in relation to the story.

Question 3: The 'correct' answer to the question is clear and unambiguous.

Instructions

In total, you will evaluate 30 scenarios. It is important to read each of them carefully and respond to the best of your ability.

Comprehension Quiz

1. Your task in this online experiment is to evaluate the quality of different short social reasoning scenarios based on the understandability of the story and whether the question and answer options make sense with respect to the story.

2. The scale for rating the understandability of a story and whether the question and answer options make sense has four response options: Disagree, Slightly Agree, Agree, and Strongly Agree.

3. Each short social reasoning scenario consists of a story, a question, and different answer options.

4. The study focuses on testing someone's social reasoning abilities using short mathematical reasoning scenarios.

5. All scenarios in this study involve a short story followed by a question with two multiple-choice response options for you to select from.
